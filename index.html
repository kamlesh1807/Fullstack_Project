Masala Dosa

It may not look like much, but street food masala dosa in Southern India is not to be underestimated.

A savoury crepe, made from fermented lentils and rice, is the perfect vessel for scooping up the accompanying sauces and sides. Fill the dosa with potatoes cooked in mustard seeds, cumin, red peppers and onion too.

Okonomiyaki: Japani

Forget famous sushi, ramen or karaage, okonomiyaki is the real deal, and the unsung hero of Japanese food.

This savoury pancake originally from Osaka is also known as “Japanese pizza” in the US, and features eggs, shredded cabbage, meat and a variety of condiments on top.

Poke: USA

Native to Hawaii, poke is made up of diced, raw fish with a range of fresh salad accompaniments.

Pronounced “pok-keh”, this dish is all about texture; think buttery, soft sashimi, partnered with the brittle crunch of veggies. And while it may seem like a fairly recent fad, it’s been on the top of Hawaiian menus for centuries.

Cannoli: Italy

This tube of goodness, thought to have originated in western Sicily, is one of Italy’s tastiest gifts.

Take a golden crunchy shell, stuff it full of creamy ricotta (sometimes you’ll find some rosewater infused too), dust it with icing sugar, and mamma mia, you have cannoli. Traditionally the sweet treat was made in spring with rich sheep’s milk ricotta.

Shakshuka: Israel

Brought to Israel from North Africa, this breaky dish makes getting up each morning a true delight.

The eggs are softly cooked in a hot skillet with herbs, peppers, tomato sauce, complex, fragrant spices, and topped with smoky, toasted pine nuts. Sop it all up with chunky bits of warm bread slathered in butter.
